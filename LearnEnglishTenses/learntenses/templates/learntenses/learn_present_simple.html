Present simple tense

Present simple is used when an action is happening right now, or when it happens regularly. Depending on the person, the present simple tense is formed by using the root form or by adding s or es to the end.

Present simple usage

  • Repeated actions
    • My friend often draws nice posters.
  • Things in general
    • The sun rises in the east.
  • Fixed arrangements, scheduled events
    • The plane flies to London every Monday.
  • Sequence of actions in the present
    • First I get up, then I have breakfast.

Signal words

Signal words are keywords or phrases that provide clues about the timing and frequency of the action described in a sentence. They help identify the tense that should be used.

  • every day
  • often
  • always
  • sometimes
  • never

Form

infinitive (3rd person singular he, she, it: infinitive + -s)
