Present perfect tense

The Present perfect tense is used for past actions that are related to or continue into the present. It's easily recognized by the auxiliary verbs have and has.

Present perfect usage

  • Result of actions in the past is important in the present â€“ It is not important when the actions happened
    • I have cleaned my room.
  • Recently completed actions
    • He has just played handball.
  • States beginning in the past and still continuing
    • We have lived in Canada since 2012.
  • Together with lately, recently, yet
    • I have been to London recently.

Signal words

Signal words are keywords or phrases that provide clues about the timing and frequency of the action described in a sentence. They help identify the tense that should be used.

  • just
  • yet
  • never
  • already
  • ever
  • so far
  • up to now
  • recently
  • since
  • for

Form

have/has + past participle*
